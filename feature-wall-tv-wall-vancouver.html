TV Feature Wall Vancouver

A TV wall often becomes the visual center of a modern living room. The challenge is making it feel intentional instead of treating the screen as an afterthought on an otherwise empty wall. Venetian plaster helps turn that surface into a more refined media wall.

What Makes a TV Wall Feel Better Designed

The strongest TV feature walls balance technology with texture. Instead of letting the screen dominate the room, the wall itself carries enough surface character and proportion to feel architectural. That is where Venetian plaster often works well.

  • Creates a calmer backdrop around the screen
  • Adds depth without busy pattern or visible seams
  • Works with shelving, millwork, and fireplace-adjacent layouts
  • Feels more premium than plain painted drywall

TV Wall Only vs TV and Fireplace Together

Some rooms need a dedicated TV wall with no fireplace at all. Others combine both elements on one elevation. A TV-only media wall can usually stay cleaner and more restrained. A combined TV-and-fireplace wall needs more composition planning so neither element feels stacked or visually disconnected.

For combined layouts, review TV Fireplace Wall Design Vancouver. For broader fireplace guidance, see Fireplace Wall Vancouver.

Where TV Feature Walls Work Best

TV feature walls are especially effective in modern living rooms, family rooms, condo great rooms, and open-plan spaces where one surface needs to anchor the full seating layout. They also work well when integrated with low cabinetry, floating shelves, or warmer minimal interiors.

For broader focal-wall planning, visit Feature Wall Vancouver and Living Room Feature Wall Vancouver.

Why Venetian Plaster Works for Media Walls

Venetian plaster softens the contrast between a black screen and the rest of the room. It gives the wall enough movement to feel custom while still staying restrained. That makes it a strong choice for homes that want a media wall to look elevated rather than overtly tech-driven.
